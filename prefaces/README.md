# CCS — Front matter (Vorspann)

OCR transcription, with English and Russian translations, of the **front matter** of:

> **Carl Cappeller**, *Sanskrit-Wörterbuch nach den Petersburger Wörterbüchern bearbeitet* — Strassburg 1887, Verlag von Karl J. Trübner (London: Trübner & Co.).

A shorter Sanskrit–German dictionary adapted from the St Petersburg dictionaries (PW/PWK), part of the [Cologne Digital Sanskrit Lexicon](https://www.sanskrit-lexicon.uni-koeln.de/) (CDSL). The front matter comprises the **title page**, a **dedication** (to Otto Böhtlingk and Rudolph Roth), and a four-page **Vorrede** (preface), signed *Jena, 3 July 1887* by Carl Cappeller.

- **Source language:** German (1887 orthography, preserved verbatim — e.g. *Theil*-era spellings, *Litteratur*, *dass*, *bloſs*, *Maſsstabe*, *That*, the long-ſ ligature in *Maſsstabe*).
- **Source scans:** Cologne csldoc, [CCS front-matter index](https://sanskrit-lexicon.uni-koeln.de/scans/csldev/csldoc/build/dictionaries/prefaces/ccspref.html).
- The tiny digitizer running-header and footer stamps that appear on every scan (*"Institute of Indology & Tamil Studies, Cologne University …"*) are **not** part of the original and have been omitted.
- Printed page numbers (Roman, VI–VIII) are kept inline as `[page N]`; the Vorrede opening page (V) carries no printed number.
- Five Devanāgarī example words appear in the Vorrede (p. VII), transcribed verbatim with their accent marks where printed: अनन्यमानस, चतुर्धा, इष्टि, स्वयुज्, कथावशेष.
